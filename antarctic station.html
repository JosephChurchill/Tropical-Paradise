Antarctic Station

ACCOMMODATION

Did you know it's dark for most of the year in Antarctica? It's the perfect spot for a mid-winter hibernation. You won't even need a sleep mask!

Our beds are Guiness certified as the coldest on the planet; so forget the ice bath and book a stay in one of our comfy tents! Your muscles will thank you.

FAUNA

Antarctica is home to some of the rarest animal species in the world, due to their inability to live anywhere warmer.

The Masked Penguin (see left) flock to the Antarctic in dozens every year for breeding, from neighbouring Macquarie Island. Their vibrant colours and raucous calls are hard to miss.

A number of other wildlife dwell here - you'll likely get the chance to pet an Antarctic Mole or an Ice Sloth.

ATTRACTIONS

Top of the list is the South Pole - the holy grail of scientists, tourists and army personell alike.

Edmund Edmundsen was the first human to set foot at the South Pole in 1903, however his pet husky Dusky is believed to have discovered the location several minutes before.

You can still see the remains of the Norwegian flag installed in the ice all those centuries ago. Come on down and experience Edmund's Legacy!

And don't forget about the glaciers! You'll probably encounter these giant natural formations at some point during your stay.

While these majestic ice structures may seem harmless, it's best to be on your guard as they might suddenly begin to charge at any moment.

In the unlikely event of an emergency, we advise you to flee the scene at your briskest pace
